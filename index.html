MITRE ATT&CK

Gap Analysis Approach

I don't write detection rules in isolation. Every rule starts from a gap — every gap starts from understanding the business.

01
Understand the Environment
Study the company's sector, data types, regulatory needs, and critical assets. A bank and a hospital face fundamentally different threats — so their detection strategy should too.
  • Industry sector & compliance requirements
  • Deployed security tools (EDR, FW, Proxy, DLP, CASB)
  • Onboarded log sources & their health
  • Existing rules & false-positive rates
02
Research Real Threats
Look at what's actually happening in the wild against similar organizations — threat reports, sector advisories, trending CVEs, APT group activity. Practical, not theoretical.
  • Sector-specific attack patterns & campaigns
  • Trending CVEs in the industry
  • APT groups targeting this vertical
03
Map to ATT&CK & Find Gaps
Map researched threats to ATT&CK techniques using the Navigator. Overlay against existing detections to surface what's uncovered — zero coverage, weak coverage, or partial detection.
  • Technique-level coverage heat map
  • Gaps vs weak vs well-covered detections
  • Prioritized by sector relevance
04
Validate Logs & Deliver Roadmap
For each gap, verify required logs actually exist. If they don't, that's a log onboarding gap — flagged separately. Deliver a prioritized report with technique, severity, log source, and approach.
  • Log availability check per gap
  • Onboarding recommendations for missing sources
  • Prioritized build roadmap for leadership
